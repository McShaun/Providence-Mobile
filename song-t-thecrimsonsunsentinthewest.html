The Crimson Sunset in the West

1. The crimson sun sets in the west, in the west outside the camp
Shoulders frail with the weight of the cross comes to mind
On his head are crown of thorns and his robe drenched in blood
With no strength, he trudges on, on this path of sacrifice

2. With one foot after the next, with each print he leaves behind
His eyes well~ up in tears, tears of blood burn in his eyes
Soldiers of the Jews and Rome, savage and without remorse
With each walk and with each step, they flog him with all their might

3. This is a path, a narrow path outside the camp of Golgotha
A path he walked shedding tears, a path he walked while shedding blood
To gain the bliss of eternal life, this is a path that we must walk
In hunger, we must still walk, and in death, we must still walk

Let’s wrap up his aching legs and heal him of injuries
He showed the way to eternal life to those who once was blind
Though we stand firm in all our trials, I will carry my own cross
Bless us so that we may climb the path of Golgotha

Though this path may be hard, though the cross is hard to bear,
This is the path the Lord has walked, so how can I not do the same
Peter, too, was crucified, upside down on the cross
It is a path I shall take, even in strife, even in death
